02. Ocean Acidification

Oceans absorb about 30% of the CO2 we produce. This changes the water's chemistry, making it harder for marine life to survive.

How it happens?

When CO2 dissolves in seawater, it forms carbonic acid, lowering the pH level of the entire ocean.

Who is affected?

Oysters,crabs and corals cannot build their protective shells, leading to an ecosystem collapse.

pH dropped by 0.1 units 30% Acidity Increase

04. Warning & Rising Tides

Oceans absorb 90% of the excess heat from global warming, causing seawater to expand and ice sheets to melt- both of which contribute to a dangerous rise in sea levels.

+1.5C

Thermal Expansion:Warm water takes up more space, which naturally causes sea levels to rise.

MELT

Glacier Retreat:Due to melting of polar ice caps, billions of tons of water are being added to the ocean every year.

Coastal Cities at Risk

By 2050, many major cities could be underwater.
